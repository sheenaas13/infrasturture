Policies and Standards

1

Corporate Governance Policy

This policy defines the corporate governance structure within InfraBuild. It establishes roles and responsibilities of the board, executive leadership, and management to ensure strategic direction, ethical conduct, and accountability.

It further promotes fairness, accountability, and transparency in decision-making processes while safeguarding the interests of shareholders, stakeholders, and the public. Through structured delegation and regular evaluations, InfraBuild ensures that its governance remains dynamic and aligned with industry standards. Governance meetings are documented thoroughly, and updates are communicated across departments to encourage collective responsibility.

2

Environmental and Sustainability Standards

This standard promotes sustainable practices by outlining clear goals for reducing emissions, minimizing waste, conserving resources, and using renewable materials.

It reinforces InfraBuild’s commitment to environmental responsibility through regulatory compliance and long-term ecological preservation strategies across all operations. The policy also emphasizes the importance of sustainable procurement and investment in green technologies. Environmental audits are conducted regularly, and outcomes are integrated into operational improvement plans. All departments receive sustainability training as part of the onboarding process.

3

Data Privacy and Protection Policy

This policy ensures that InfraBuild collects, stores, and processes data in a secure and ethical manner. It mandates data encryption, access controls, and regular audits.

It also supports a culture of data stewardship where all employees are trained to recognize and respond to potential data threats and breaches swiftly and responsibly. The policy includes procedures for breach notifications and data subject rights. It is reviewed semi-annually to keep pace with evolving regulatory landscapes and digital threats. InfraBuild partners with certified vendors to ensure data compliance throughout the supply chain.

4

Procurement and Vendor Management Guidelines

This framework outlines procedures for fair vendor selection, transparent bidding, and ongoing performance evaluation.

It helps ensure value for money, compliance with laws, and ethical standards while building a competitive, reliable, and diverse supplier ecosystem. Vendor performance is monitored via scorecards and on-site inspections. Long-term vendor partnerships are built on mutual respect, shared goals, and compliance with InfraBuild's code of conduct. The policy encourages support for local suppliers and businesses aligned with sustainable practices.

5

Health, Safety, and Wellbeing Framework

This framework creates a proactive safety culture by enforcing industry-leading health and safety practices including training and emergency preparedness.

It also encourages wellbeing initiatives, mental health support, and fitness programs to create a healthy and resilient workforce. Each site has dedicated safety officers and regular safety drills. Employees receive access to confidential health services, and work-life balance is promoted through flexible work arrangements. Management tracks health indicators to proactively improve wellbeing outcomes.

6

Risk Management Protocols

This protocol provides a systematic approach to identifying and mitigating risks related to operations, compliance, finance, and reputation.

It ensures timely decision-making and preparedness by leveraging risk heatmaps, periodic reviews, and crisis simulation exercises. Risk ownership is clearly defined, and reporting frameworks are embedded into all departments. Incident response teams are trained and regularly assessed. InfraBuild's board is briefed quarterly on enterprise risk trends and emerging vulnerabilities.

7

Ethical Conduct and Anti-Corruption Standards

This policy ensures ethical behavior across all InfraBuild activities. It prohibits bribery, fraud, and conflicts of interest, and promotes whistleblower protections.

InfraBuild maintains a zero-tolerance policy on corruption and reinforces this through annual ethics training and an independent grievance redressal system. All employees and partners are required to sign ethical compliance forms. An internal ethics committee oversees investigations and policy enforcement. The company’s integrity is a cornerstone of its public image and industry leadership.

8

Stakeholder Engagement and Communication Policy

This policy establishes guidelines for open, honest, and respectful dialogue with stakeholders, including communities and regulators.

It encourages transparency and collaboration through community briefings, regular reporting, digital channels, and stakeholder feedback sessions. Stakeholder concerns are logged and monitored using CRM tools. InfraBuild actively collaborates with local governments and NGOs to align infrastructure development with public needs. Educational campaigns ensure informed participation in public consultations.

9

Project Lifecycle and Quality Assurance Standards

This standard governs the entire project lifecycle from planning to completion, ensuring every stage meets defined benchmarks.

It fosters consistency and reliability in infrastructure delivery by integrating technical audits, quality reviews, and documentation best practices. Key performance indicators are tracked from initiation to handover. The policy supports third-party validations and post-project reviews. Quality awareness is instilled through mandatory training for all project team members.
